# Evidence Locker — Colonialism Series

**Archiving colonialism as a global system — Africa, Indochina, North America, South America.**

---

## What This Is

A living archive documenting:
- **Colonialism as a connected system** — not isolated events, but common mechanisms of extraction, dispossession, and control
- **The legal and ideological foundations** — Doctrine of Discovery, treaties, fraudulent agreements
- **The human cost** — death tolls, forced removals, cultural erasure
- **The continuity** — how colonial patterns evolved into modern debt, surveillance, and infrastructure

---

## Why This Exists

This is not abstract history. It is **family history** — Irish, Native American, Black, French Canadian — and the evidence that connects colonial violence to contemporary power structures.

**The premise:** Colonialism didn't end in the 1960s-70s. It evolved into economic, military, and informational frameworks that maintain the same power asymmetries under different names.
