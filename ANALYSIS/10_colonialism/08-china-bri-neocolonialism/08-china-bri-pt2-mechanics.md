## 2.2 What the BRI Actually Does

The BRI is not a single project but a sprawling framework of infrastructure, trade, and investment agreements. The name itself reveals its ambition: the "Belt" revives overland Silk Road corridors through Central Asia, while the "Road" rekindles maritime trade routes from Southeast Asia to Africa.

It operates through:

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Bilateral MOUs** | Memoranda of understanding with partner countries — 150+ signed as of 2025 |
| **State-owned enterprise (SOE) projects** | Chinese state firms (e.g., China Railway Construction Corp, Power Construction Corp) execute infrastructure projects |
| **Policy bank financing** | China Development Bank, Export-Import Bank of China provide long-term loans |
| **Multilateral institutions** | Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB), Silk Road Fund provide additional financing |
| **Private sector involvement** | CATL, Zijin Mining, Ganfeng Lithium — Chinese private firms expanding overseas |

The initiative has been formally aligned with China's constitutional principles and elevated as a core component of Chinese national development strategy.

## 2.3 The Six Economic Corridors

The BRI is organized around six overland economic corridors:

| Corridor | Route | Key Projects |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| **China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC)** | China → Pakistan | Gwadar Port, motorways, power plants; launched 2015; $25B+ invested in Phase 1 |
| **China-Central Asia-West Asia** | China → Central Asia → West Asia | Cross-Caspian freight route, Kazakhstan–China rail links |
| **New Eurasian Land Bridge** | China → Kazakhstan → Russia → Europe | China-Europe freight trains; $426B+ in goods transported by end of 2024 |
| **China-Mongolia-Russia** | China → Mongolia → Russia | Resource corridors |
| **China-Indochina Peninsula** | China → Southeast Asia | China-Laos Railway (operational 2021); Jakarta-Bandung High-Speed Rail |
| **Maritime Silk Road** | China → Southeast Asia → Africa → Europe | Port of Piraeus (Greece), Gwadar, Hambantota |

## 2.5 Sectoral Distribution

China's 2025 BRI surge shows a clear strategic pivot:

| Sector | 2025 Investment | Significance |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Energy** | $93.9 billion | Largest sector — oil, gas, renewables |
| **Metals & Mining** | $32.6 billion | Lithium, copper, rare earths — critical minerals for EV revolution |
| **Construction contracts (H1 2025)** | $66.2 billion | Infrastructure build-out |
| **Non-financial direct investment (2025)** | $35.7 billion | Equity stakes, acquisitions |

The Middle East has emerged as a primary destination, with **cumulative BRI investments in the region now at approximately $3.5 trillion**. Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Egypt, and the UAE are the largest recipients.
