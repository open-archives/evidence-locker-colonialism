## Executive Summary

The colonial project in North America didn't end with the closure of residential schools. It **evolved** — into foster care removal (Sixties Scoop, ongoing), extractive industries on reservations, legal battles over treaty rights, and the epidemic of Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women and Girls (MMIP). The **Land Back movement** and **sovereignty battles** represent contemporary resistance. Meanwhile, the U.S. still has not issued a formal apology for residential schools, and Canada has only partially implemented the Truth and Reconciliation Commission's 94 Calls to Action.

**Key Finding:** The mechanisms of dispossession — land theft, cultural erasure, child removal — continue in modern forms. The resistance is alive, but the structural power imbalances remain largely intact.

## Part 1: Timeline — Modern Legacies (1960s–2026)

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **1968** | American Indian Movement (AIM) founded | Militant resistance; occupation of Alcatraz (1969), Wounded Knee (1973) |
| **1978** | Indian Child Welfare Act (ICWA) passed (U.S.) | Attempt to stop removal of Native children; still under legal attack |
| **1980s–present** | Extractive industries on reservations | Coal, uranium, oil, gas — poverty persists while resources flow out |
| **1990** | Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA) | Requires return of human remains and sacred objects |
| **2008** | Canada apologizes for residential schools | Harper apology; TRC established |
| **2015** | TRC Final Report; 94 Calls to Action | Landmark document; implementation still incomplete |
| **2016–2026** | MMIP awareness grows | Thousands of Indigenous women missing/murdered; under-investigated |
| **2020** | Land Back movement gains traction | Indigenous-led campaigns for return of ancestral lands |
| **2021** | Kamloops GPR anomalies discovered | Triggers renewed national reckoning in Canada |
| **2026** | U.S. still has no federal apology for boarding schools | Some states have issued apologies; federal level stalled |
