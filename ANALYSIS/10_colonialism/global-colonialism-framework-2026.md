## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This series examines colonialism not as isolated historical events but as a global system connecting disparate regions through common mechanisms of extraction, dispossession, and control. From Berlin Conference border-drawing to modern debt structures, the patterns persist across centuries and continents.

**Key Premise:** Colonialism didn't "end" in the 1960s-70s — it evolved into economic, military, and informational frameworks that maintain the same power asymmetries under different names.
